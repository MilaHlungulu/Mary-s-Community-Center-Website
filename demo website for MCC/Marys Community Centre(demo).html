Our Story

Mary’s Community Centre was founded in 2000 and registered as a non-profit organization (NPO 028-418) in 2003. It was established by women affected by HIV/AIDS to provide home-based care for the chronically ill and to address stigma and discrimination.

Over time, our programs have grown to include TB DOT support, tracing of patients who default on medication, and advocacy with the Department of Health. We adapt continually to meet the evolving needs of our community.

Vision: Strengthening community action to address HIV stigma among young people, vulnerable, and key populations by 2023.

Mission: Achieving a free HIV-positive country through increased HIV counselling and testing by 2025.

Values: Honesty, Transparency, Collaboration, People-Oriented, Creative, Trustworthy.

Our Programs

We offer a variety of programs designed to empower individuals and strengthen communities across South Africa

HIV Oral Self-Testing

Distribution of 16,000 self-test kits over 7 months with WhatsApp and phone follow-ups.

  • Door-to-door and fixed point distribution
  • Supported by WHRI

HIV Counseling & Testing (HIVSA)

80% of clients linked to same-day CD4 testing and 90% reach viral suppression.

Adherence Club

Serving adolescents, sex workers, transgender, and MSM groups for 2 years with 99 active members.

Orphans & Vulnerable Children

Psychosocial support, sports, nutrition, homework, career guidance, and mentoring.

Elderly Wellness Club

Home visits, caring for the sick, and club wellness activities for senior citizens.

Family Re-unification & Rehabilitation

Support for homeless, drug users, and refugees through family tracing, skills and documentation.

Poverty Alleviation

Cooking lunch for community members four days a week with Feedforward support.
